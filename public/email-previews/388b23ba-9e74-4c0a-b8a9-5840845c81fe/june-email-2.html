The Bank of Canada's June 10 decision changes the conversation for alternative borrowers in a specific way. Let me be direct about what that looks like.

For clients currently on a B-lender product, working toward A qualification:

B-lender rates are not tied directly to the Bank of Canada rate in the same way as conventional lenders. B-lenders price for risk, and their rates carry a broader spread. A BoC cut does not automatically move your B-lender rate. What it does change is your path back to A.

If today's announcement was a rate cut, conventional lenders' qualifying rates may soften in the coming weeks. That's meaningful if you've been rebuilding credit and waiting for the qualifying threshold to come down to meet you.

If today's announcement was a hold, the math stays where it was. That doesn't mean nothing changes. It means you keep building, and the conversation in three to six months may look different than it does today.

For clients in private lending, the BoC decision matters less on paper, but the secondary effect matters a great deal. What happens to A and B lenders in the months that follow determines how quickly a private client can move back into mainstream lending. That's a timeline worth planning for now.
