Why I stopped competing with the bank

Hi {{contact.first_name}},

There's an honest version of what happened with my business this spring, and I want to share it with the people I trust.

The banks are offering rates on prime A-borrower renewals that I cannot beat. If you have clean credit, stable T4 employment, and you're renewing with your current lender, they have a rate right now that I simply cannot match. I've tried. It's not a winnable fight on those terms.

So I changed the terms.

Fuse Mortgage is going all-in on alternative lending. Bruised credit. High debt ratios. Self-employed income that doesn't read clean on a tax return. Separating couples navigating complex title situations. Newcomers who arrived two years ago with no Canadian credit history yet. These are the clients the bank turns away, and they are the clients I am best positioned to help.

Alternative lending is not a compromise. It's a specialty. The brokers who do it well know which B-lenders and private lenders are right for which situations, how to structure the file for the best possible terms, and how to build a clear path back to A qualification when the client is ready.

I've spent the past few months going deeper on exactly this. If you have referral partners who send you the hard files, I would like to be your call when those files arrive. If you have a client the bank just said no to, let's talk.
