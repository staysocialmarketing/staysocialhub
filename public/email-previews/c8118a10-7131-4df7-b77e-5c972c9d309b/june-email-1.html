What your money actually buys in NL right now

It's summer in Newfoundland, and the market is moving in ways worth talking about.

Let me give you the actual picture, not the provincial headlines.

In St. John's, $350,000 to $420,000 is where most of the action is right now. You're looking at solid homes in Mount Pearl, Paradise, and CBS. Good neighbourhoods, established communities, the kind of place where your kids can walk to school. Properties in good condition are moving. Properties that need work are sitting longer.

In Gander, $250,000 still buys you something substantial. A three-bedroom detached with a yard and room to breathe. The central Newfoundland market has been steady — not wild, not slow. Buyers who move without hesitation are getting what they're looking for.

Corner Brook is similar. For buyers who have flexibility on location, the affordability gap between St. John's and the rest of the province is worth considering. You're getting significantly more home for your money.

What I keep telling people: Newfoundland is still one of the most affordable places in Canada to buy a home. That won't be true forever. It's been said before and people didn't listen, and then they wished they had. If you're thinking about buying this summer, let's have a real conversation about what's possible.
