Self-employed mortgage qualification: the full breakdown
 

Hi {{contact.first_name}},

The self-employed mortgage conversation is one I have every week. Here's the full picture — because most explanations leave half of it out.

Toronto business owners generally fall into one of four scenarios.

T1 generals only. If you're a sole proprietor filing a T1, lenders use your net business income after expenses. The more you've written off, the lower the income that qualifies. This is the most common friction point.

Corporate structure with a salary. If your corporation pays you a T4, lenders treat that salary like employment income. Clean, straightforward. But there's often more in the corporation that could strengthen your application if it's positioned correctly.

Corporate structure with retained earnings. Some lenders will include retained earnings in the income calculation. Not all. Knowing which lenders are open to this — and how to document it properly — is often the difference between a declined file and an approved one.

Stated income programs. For clients whose income doesn't fit the standard model, some lenders offer stated income products with specific qualifying criteria. These aren't a workaround. They're a legitimate product, used correctly.

The most common thing I hear from self-employed clients: "I was told my income is too complicated." It's not complicated. It's different.
