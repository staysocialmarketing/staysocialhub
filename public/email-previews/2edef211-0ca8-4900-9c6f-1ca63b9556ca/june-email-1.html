Condition-free offers: the math nobody shows you
 

Hi {{contact.first_name}},

Here's the number your Realtor may not have mentioned.

In a condition-free offer, the lender mortgages on the lower of the appraised value or your purchase price. Not the price you agreed to pay. The lower number.

If those two numbers match, there's no problem. If they don't, the gap is your problem.

Here's what that looks like in three real scenarios.

At $500,000: you offer $500K condition-free. The lender appraises at $480K. The mortgage is based on $480K. You need to find $20,000 in cash to close, on top of your down payment.

At $650,000: you offer $650K condition-free. The lender appraises at $620K. You need $30,000 in cash to close, on top of your down payment, on top of closing costs. Most buyers in Durham Region don't have that sitting in their account.

At $850,000: you offer $850K. The lender appraises at $810K. You need $40,000 in cash to close. At 5% down, that's more than your original down payment.

This is not a hypothetical. It happens. And when it happens without conditions in place, there is no protection.
