The Bank of Canada made its June 10 call. Here's what it means from Sudbury, not Bay Street.

Two scenarios from clients I work with regularly.

A mine worker at Glencore, buying at $320,000 in Garson. Income is solid but it comes in shifts, variable by rotation and bonus structure. A rate change in either direction moves the payment calculation, but what I care about more is whether the mortgage structure fits the way that income actually arrives. A variable rate that looks good on announcement day can create stress during a slow rotation. That's the conversation we have before the rate decision, not after.

A teacher in the Sudbury Catholic District, renewing a $280,000 mortgage in New Sudbury. Income is stable, employment is pensioned, the renewal window is here. For this client, today's announcement matters because fixed versus variable looks different than it did in March. Running the comparison on the actual balance, at the actual renewal date, with the actual lender options available is a thirty-minute conversation worth having.

The rate decision is public information. What it means for your mortgage is specific to your file.

Is this the right mortgage for your life? That's still the only question that matters.
