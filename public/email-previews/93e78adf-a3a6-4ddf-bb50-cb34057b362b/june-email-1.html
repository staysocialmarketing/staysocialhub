If you've been watching the headlines about Ontario housing, most of what you're reading isn't about you.

The Ontario average is pulled north by the GTA, and it doesn't tell you anything useful about what's happening on Regent Street or in Valley East.

Here's what $350,000 looks like in Sudbury this summer.

$300,000 to $350,000: solid three-bedroom home in Garson, Lively, or Chelmsford. Good bones, real space, a neighbourhood where people know their neighbours. These aren't starter flats — they're family homes.

$350,000 to $400,000: Minnow Lake, Capreol, or parts of New Sudbury, where inventory moves quickly and properties in good condition rarely sit more than two or three weeks.

Over $400,000: renovated homes on the south end, newer builds in Lively, or properties with acreage outside the city. These move a little slower, and that's not a bad thing.

The pattern I've been watching: Sudbury families who know what they want are moving with more confidence this spring than they were a year ago. The buyers who hesitate the longest are usually the ones comparing themselves to Toronto.
