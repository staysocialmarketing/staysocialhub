Hi {{contact.first_name}},

June is Pride Month. This year I wanted to write you a letter about something that matters to me, not as a tagline, but as the reason I do this work the way I do it.

A couple came to my office a few years ago. They'd been to another broker first. They left that meeting feeling like they had to explain themselves before they could ask a single question about their mortgage. They were a same-sex couple, and from the moment they walked in, they felt like the broker was working out how to fit them into a form that wasn't designed for them.

I want to be the broker who was designed for you, whoever you are.

When two partners apply together, there are questions that matter: how titling works, how joint ownership is structured, how to protect both people in the application. Those are not complicated questions. But they require a broker who asks them naturally, as a matter of course, not as a concession.

That couple closed on their home six months after our first conversation. I was there at the closing. We all cried a little.

If you've been waiting to find the right person to have this conversation with, I'm here. No judgment. No assumptions. Just someone who genuinely wants to help you find your home, with whoever you're building it.
