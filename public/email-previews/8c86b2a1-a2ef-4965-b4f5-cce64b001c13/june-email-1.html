Pre-approval doesn't mean what you think
 

Hi {{contact.first_name}},

Most buyers in Halifax go into a pre-approval conversation thinking it's the last hard conversation they'll need to have. It isn't.

A pre-approval tells you what a lender is willing to do based on what they know today. It does not lock your rate until you have a firm purchase agreement. It does not account for what changes between now and closing. In the $350,000 to $500,000 range in Halifax right now, those changes happen more often than buyers expect.

Here's what can shift between pre-approval and closing.

Your rate. If rates move between your pre-approval and your rate hold date, and you haven't locked in, you're qualifying against a new number. Most pre-approvals carry a 90 to 120-day rate hold. If your search runs long, that window closes.

Your employment. Pre-approval is based on your income at the time of application. A job change, even a promotion, can trigger a new assessment. Lenders want to see stability.

The property itself. Pre-approval is about you. Final approval is also about the property. A non-standard build, a rural location, or a foundation issue can introduce conditions the pre-approval never anticipated.
