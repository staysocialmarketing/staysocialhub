The Atlantic-to-Ontario move is one of the most common decisions I help clients navigate, and almost nobody has a clear picture of what actually changes in their mortgage qualification when they cross provincial lines.

Here's what most people expect: they bring their pre-approval from Nova Scotia, apply at an Ontario price point, and adjust their budget. Here's what most people don't expect: the qualification works differently in ways that have nothing to do with the rate.

Two things shift materially when you move from Atlantic Canada to Ontario.

First, the price point changes the stress test calculation in a meaningful way. A buyer qualifying for $420,000 in Halifax may bring the same income and same down payment to an Ontario purchase and find the stress test ceiling sits lower than they assumed. The qualifying rate is fixed, but debt service ratios stretch differently at $750,000 versus $420,000.

Second, lenders read employment history differently when you've relocated. If the move happened in the last 12 to 24 months, the documentation requirements vary depending on whether you're employed, self-employed, or transitioning roles. Some lenders want to see continuity in the same field. Others are flexible. I know which is which.

I've positioned files on both sides of this move. If you or a client is planning a relocation, that conversation is worth having before the search begins. You're not just buying in your city. You're making a decision across markets that don't move the same way.
