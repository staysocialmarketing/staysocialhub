### Seasonal Trends

Based on Ontario and broader Canadian rental market data:

- **Peak search season:** Spring (March–June) and late summer (July–August) — when leases turn over and new movers are active
- **Lowest activity:** December–January — seasonal dip in rental searches across Canada
- **University/college effect:** Cornwall has St. Lawrence College — minor August–September student demand spike, but not The Johnson's primary audience
- **Rate-cut effect (2025–2026):** Lower interest rates are driving some renters to consider buying, but supply shortfalls keep rental demand strong in smaller Ontario markets
- **Recommendation:** Front-load ad spend in spring (March–June) and late summer (July–August). Reduce budget to maintenance level ($200–300/mo) in Nov–Jan.

## 2. Competitive Analysis

### What Competitors Are Doing

Cornwall's rental competition is primarily older buildings and private landlords listing on:
- Kijiji (dominant local platform — classified-style, no brand)
- Facebook Marketplace (price-led, photo-heavy, minimal copy)
- Apartments.com, Rentals.ca, Zumper (aggregators — functional, no differentiation)
- A Plus Management (local property management company with some portfolio properties)

**Competitor marketing angles observed:**
- Price-first listings ("$1,450/mo — 2BR near downtown")
- Utility callouts ("heat and water included") — typically on older stock
- Pet-friendly callouts (89% of Cornwall listings allow pets — a crowd)
- Location references ("near hospital," "close to 401")
- Stock photography or low-quality phone photos

### What's NOT Being Said — The Johnson's Ownable Territory

No Cornwall rental is currently marketing:

1. **The bundle as a financial argument** — no one is doing the math: "At $2,280, with parking, water, and in-unit laundry included, you're paying less per included feature than most buildings charging $1,600 with coin laundry and paid parking."
2. **Modern build quality as a category** — the market defaults to commodity listings; no one owns "new construction quality"
3. **Ottawa comparison** — zero Cornwall landlords are targeting Ottawa-priced-out renters with paid ads
4. **Private outdoor space** — balconies/patios are rare in Cornwall rental stock; no one is leading with this
5. **Lifestyle aspiration** — every listing is transactional. No one is selling the *feeling* of living there
6. **"No surprises" pricing transparency** — Cornwall renters are conditioned to budget uncertainty; The Johnson's all-in pricing is a differentiator no one else is claiming
